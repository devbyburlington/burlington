Installation Guide

Gmail

1
Find your signature on this page and click "Copy Signature"
2
Open Gmail → click the gear icon (top right) → "See all settings"
3
Scroll down to the "Signature" section under the General tab
4
Click "+ Create new" and give it a name (e.g. "Burlington")
5
Click inside the signature text box and paste (Ctrl+V / Cmd+V). The formatted signature should appear with your photo, teal divider, and button.
6
Under "Signature defaults", set your new signature for both "For new emails" and "On reply/forward"
7
Scroll to the bottom and click "Save Changes"

Outlook (Web)

1
Copy your signature from this page
2
Open Outlook → click the gear icon → "View all Outlook settings"
3
Go to Mail → Compose and reply
4
Under "Email signature", click "+ New signature" and name it
5
Paste into the editor (Ctrl+V / Cmd+V)
6
Set as default for new messages and replies, then Save

Outlook (Desktop — Mac/Windows)

1
Copy your signature from this page
2
Mac: Outlook → Settings → Signatures → click "+" to add new
Windows: File → Options → Mail → Signatures → New
3
Paste into the signature editor and save. Set as default for new messages and replies.

Apple Mail (Mac)

1
Copy your signature from this page
2
Open Mail → Settings (Cmd+,) → Signatures tab
3
Select your email account on the left, then click "+" to add a new signature
4
Paste into the right-hand preview panel (Cmd+V)
5
Set the "Choose Signature" dropdown to your new signature for that account

iPhone / iPad (iOS Mail)

1
Open this page on your phone: burlingtonconsult.com/signatures
2
Tap "Copy Signature" on your signature
3
Go to Settings → Mail → Signature
4
Tap the signature field, select all existing text, and paste
*
Note: iOS Mail has limited HTML support. The signature will work but may render with simplified formatting. For the best result, set up via Gmail or Outlook on desktop.

Troubleshooting

?
Signature shows as raw code: Make sure you're pasting with Ctrl+V / Cmd+V into the visual editor, not a source/code view. The copy button copies rich formatted content.
?
Photo not showing: The headshot is hosted on burlingtonconsult.com — it loads from the web each time the email is opened. Some email clients block images by default; recipients can click "show images" to see it.
?
"Book Consultation" button not clickable: Some email clients render the button as plain text. This is normal — the link still works when clicked in the sent email.
?
Need to update your details? Contact the engineering team or edit the signature directly in your email client's settings.
